By Steve Benjamins, Juhil Mendpara Updated Jan 3 2026 I’ve published my rankings of the best website builders every year since 2014. Year upon year, including this time, Squarespace and Shopify have proved to be the top website builders. Wix and Webflow have followed closely each time, including in 2024. [And inevitably, many have lost their rankings, and some have disappeared into oblivion.] This is your ultimate guide—and my rankings—to the best website builder in 2026. I’ve interviewed hundreds of real-life users, manage several websites made with website builders, and have personally tested all website builders in this guide (and many, many more). My websites have been featured on HuffPost, Forbes, CNET, The Next Web, and more. Let’s get started… Our Methodology: Choosing The Best Website Builder After reviewing and ranking website builders for almost a decade, talking to hundreds of customers, assessing thousands of websites made with different website builders, and being on the other side of choosing a website builder, we have a good idea of what people want. Our user-focused tests and rankings are based on four pillars—in priority order: Ease Of Use And Customizability — People choose a website builder to make a website fast without requiring coding experience. So we looked for website builders that are simple to use but also offer enough customizability to make your dream site. This involved assessing onboarding, page editor experience, and template quality and variety. Features — If the website builder doesn’t have the features and functionality you need, it isn’t a suitable website builder. Therefore, our assessment focused on core features like mobile responsiveness, SSL security, SEO tools, and storage and bandwidth. We also considered other widely-used features like blogging, ecommerce, multimedia support, galleries, contact forms, connecting custom domains from third-party registrars, business emails, and social media integrations. Naturally, this general-purpose website builder guide doesn’t concentrate on or give equal weightage to niche features like live video streaming, AR integration, etc. Value for Money — We evaluated the cost-effectiveness of each website builder by comparing their pricing plans against the features they offer, aiming to find options that provide maximum value. This evaluation also included checking for free trials and free plans. Note: The first two factors trump “price” for obvious reasons. Help Docs and Customer Support — You won’t just pick up a website builder and know everything about it. You’ll likely have questions when making the website and operating the website builder. Those questions are into two categories: General and Specific. Answers to general questions can often be found in help docs/videos or communities, whereas specific questions require human help. So we looked at both—we evaluated the range and clarity of help documents and videos and tested the responsiveness of customer service. Besides, we took note of real-user reviews and complaints across the internet (like on Reddit), each company’s billing practices, the history of the companies locking/deleting websites, etc. This approach helped us recommend website builders that offer a balance of usability, features, value, and support.
