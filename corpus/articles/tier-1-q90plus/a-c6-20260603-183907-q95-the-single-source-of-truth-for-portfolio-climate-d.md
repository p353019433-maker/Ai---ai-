Read the announcement Trusted by global asset owners The operational burden of climate reporting Sourcing holdings from multiple investment sources, managing disconnected climate sources, and unifying public and private markets creates an immense operational burden. Investment data silos Holdings information is trapped in silos across custodians, fund administrators, internal systems, and spreadsheets. This fragmentation spans both public and private markets, making reconciliation a manual engineering feat. Climate data fragmentation Teams receive feeds in differing formats, at different times, covering different reporting periods. Normalizing this disparate data for consistent portfolio usage is a constant struggle. Reporting complexity Stitching together public and private data, calculating attribution percentages across multi-layered structures, and preparing data for scenario analysis requires complex logic that spreadsheets struggle to handle. The annual reset Without a persistent data platform, the entire collection and reconciliation process often restarts from scratch each year, making it difficult to track and explain year-on-year movements. New collaboration required Mandatory reporting demands a new level of collaboration between Finance and Responsible Investment teams to reconcile sustainability data with financial ledgers and Net Asset Value (NAV). Looming mandatory reporting As jurisdictions like Australia enforce mandatory climate disclosure, teams face pressure to prepare for assurance-ready reporting despite uncertainty around specific requirements and standards.
