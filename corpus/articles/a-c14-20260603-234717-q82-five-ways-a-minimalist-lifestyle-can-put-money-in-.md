Opinions expressed are those of the author. | Membership (fee-based)Oct 03, 2017, 09:00am EDTOct 03, 2017, 09:00am EDT Attaining wealth isn't always about how much money you make. Often, it's about how much money you save through your lifestyle choices. There's a saying that goes, "it takes money to make money." With that in mind, saving up enough money so that you are able to put that money to work is highly important. Shutterstock To help you start saving more of the money you earn, here are five ways that a minimalist lifestyle can put money into your pocket: 1. Purchase things that have real value. Most items we purchase begin to decrease in value the second we assume ownership of them. Cars, clothes, electronics and more are immediately worth less than we paid for them as soon as the transaction takes place, and their value almost always continues to decrease for the duration that we own them. While many of these items are necessary, it's important to purchase things that have real value when you can. These things include purchases such as real estate, stocks and bonds, commodities and other such investments that retain or even increase their value over time. 2. Don't buy the latest and greatest. Too many people fall into the trap of upgrading the items they own at every chance they get. They buy new cars when their old ones are still running fine and new phones when their old ones are barely even a year old. The companies selling these products count on customers upgrading to the latest and greatest, and their entire marketing strategy depends on convincing customers they need to do so. The truth is that constantly upgrading the products you own is a waste of money and guarantees that you will only be momentarily satisfied with your purchase. Learn to use and enjoy a product for its full life cycle and you'll be much better off in the end. 3. Clean the clutter from your life. Clutter is costly. Products, activities and habits that you don't really enjoy and that don't really offer any value can clutter up your life and act as a drain on your finances. Take a look at the things in your life, and examine if they are really worth keeping around. For most people, there are a number of activities and products that we spend our money on almost entirely out of habit rather than necessity or even enjoyment. Cut these expenses out and you almost certainly won't miss them. 4. Buy used or pre-owned. We've already mentioned that the price of a new product takes a big hit the second it is purchased. The good news, though, is that you can actually benefit from this price drop if you buy lightly used products. In many cases, lightly used products are almost indistinguishable from a product that is brand new, save for a considerable difference in price. This difference in price is one that many people are willing to pay just for the idea that they own a virgin product. Yet if you get past this mentality, buying used is really the better deal. 5. Don't worry about appearances. Did you know that even after Sam Walton, the founder of Walmart, became one of the wealthiest men in the world, he still drove around a 1979 Ford F-150 that looked like something a small-town farmer would drive, rather than a vehicle owned by a multi-billionaire? Sam Walton didn't worry about using his money to flaunt his status and neither should you. Keeping up with the Joneses is unnecessary and expensive, and if you are purchasing a product simply because of its image, then you should think twice. Your possessions don't define you and rarely are they able to change other people's opinions about you. If you learn to live a lifestyle that isn't concerned with appearances, you'll not only save a lot of money, but you'll be much happier, too. In Conclusion Once you start adjusting your lifestyle choices, you will notice the difference in your savings. Even the smallest changes to your spending habits will begin to add up and, before long, you will have enough money to make your first investment. When you get to that point, you may want to know how much of your newfound funds should be saved, spent and invested, so I created a video that answers the most common questions I receive on the matter to help you get started. Remember that by adopting minimalist lifestyle tendencies and strategically planning for your future, anyone may achieve the level of financial freedom they desire. You must simple harness control of your saving, spending and investing.
