Photo by Tom Rossiter Gothic Heritage Though the Regenstein Library is a work of Brutalist architecture, it honors the Gothic tradition by using positive and negative space in its mass to play with light and shadow. Photo by Tom Rossiter Intricate Stair Well The library’s stairs reflect the building’s exterior massing. Photo by Tom Rossiter Negative Space Architect Walter Netsch spoke of the “negative spaces,” voids in the mass of the building. Photo by Tom Rossiter Completed: 1970 Architect: Skidmore, Owings & Merrill Address: 1100 E. 57th St. Google Map Known for its Brutalist architecture and central campus location, Regenstein Library has served as a key part of student life since its opening in 1970. Integral Facilities Boasting 577,085 square feet, Regenstein is the largest library on campus. The reinforced concrete building features seven stories and a mechanical penthouse. It utilizes compact shelving on its B-level to maximize its storage capacities and house its diverse collections. The outer wall of the library projects and recedes, creates partial courtyards, and introduces the play of light and shadow in the manner of Gothic architecture.
