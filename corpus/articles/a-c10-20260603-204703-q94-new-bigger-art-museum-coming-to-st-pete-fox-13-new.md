PETERSBURG (FOX 13) - St. Petersburg's bustling art community is about to get even bigger. Construction fencing now surrounds the site of St. Pete's next, and largest, arts museum. The Museum of the American Arts and Crafts Movement is expected to open in 2017. "It's a $40 million project,” said Wayne Stherholt, the city cultural affairs director. "It's probably the best collection of American Arts and Crafts artifacts that exists, so it's a very important museum." Pinellas entrepreneur Rudy Ciccareello assembled the artifacts and is now building and endowing their new home. "Gosh, I think we're all just grateful that this one individual is doing this in St. Petersburg," Jeff Schorr said. Schorr owns the Craftsman House Gallery on Central Avenue, which show cases artists carrying on a style dating back to the industrial revolution. "Whether it was a chair, or a cutting board or a tile or a vase, let's make it beautiful, let's not make a hundred of these as quickly as we can in an assembly line," Schorr said. "Let's make these by hand and make it functional and beautiful." The new museum will be nearly twice the size of the Salvador Dali Museum. "There's furniture, there's sculpture, these are some big pieces, it's not like two dimensional art that's on the wall" said Atherholt. Renderings on architect Albert Alfonso's web site suggests many of the artifacts will be displayed in rooms, showing how they were actually used in the early 1900's. (More information on the museum can be found at tworedroses.com.) When it opens in 2017, it is expected to be yet another attraction for the tourism industry. "We've got the Dali, we've got the Chihuly, we've got the Museum of Fine Arts, we've got untold numbers of galleries and crafts" Pinellas tourism director David Downing observed. "This is a growing industry; this is what separates the beach destination like ours from other destinations."
