## Main additions

### Brand, sustainable design, interface, and research-based design

Recovered Wikipedia/basic-reference rows for brand, sustainable design, user interface, graphical widgets, HUDs, mockups, research-based design, and design computing. These are foundational definitions, not deep theory, but they close important vocabulary gaps.

Rules:

- Brand is a system of recognition, promise, differentiation, and memory; do not reduce it to identity graphics.
- Sustainable design is lifecycle thinking, not green visual styling.
- UI is a control/feedback/representation layer between human intention and machine/system behavior.
- Research-based design turns evidence into constraints and evaluation, not just moodboard legitimacy.

### Typography and color infrastructure

Recovered Times New Roman, Didot, Fira, Hershey fonts, Natural Color System, secondary color, shades of green/orange/gray, and Mother of All HTML Color Charts. These are partly low-level reference rows, but they support typography/color infrastructure.

Rules:

- Typefaces carry production history: newspaper economy, screen rendering, vector/stroke systems, open licensing, and institutional voice.
- Color systems differ: perceptual systems, web/hex references, named-color taxonomies, and cultural associations are not interchangeable.
- Color charts are reference infrastructure, not design judgment by themselves.

### Graphic systems and visual culture

Recovered Paula Scher, Michael Bierut/Times Square, The Design of Symbols, packaging/film Imprint topics, Edward McKnight Kauffer, Kerr Noble, M/M, Timorous Beasties, The Guardian, Abram-Games-adjacent and related graphic topics.

Rules:

- Environmental graphics turn place into legible public experience.
- Symbols compress action, category, and memory; they fail when compression outruns recognition.
- Graphic authorship can live in campaigns, editorial systems, packaging, film, fashion, and institutional wayfinding.

### Architecture, planning, and environmental design

Recovered Philip Johnson, Alvar Aalto, Giancarlo De Carlo, Henning Larsen, architects’ summer retreats, Buildings & Gardens, wayfinding, environmental design, permeability, urban planning in Nazi Germany, design science, and Salone del Mobile.

Rules:

- Architecture history should be read as system of patronage, ideology, movement, site, technology, and social program.
- Wayfinding/permeability are behavioral properties of space.
- Design science and design computing connect design to method, modeling, and systematic inquiry.
- Design fairs/salons are market-attention infrastructures, not neutral showcases.

### Product/material/fashion/industrial design

Recovered Jean Prouvé, Achille Castiglioni, Jaguar, Biba, Ambrose Heal, Philip Treacy, Flaminio Bertoni, Ronan/Erwan Bouroullec, John Galliano, Established & Sons, Phyllis Pearsall, Solange Azagury-Partridge, Enzo Mari, Paul Cocksedge, Konstantin Grcic, aluminium, plywood, J. Mays, Danish design, sensory design, and others.

Rules:

- Materials such as aluminium and plywood are design enablers; they change possible forms, weights, costs, and production methods.
- Product design often turns around one decisive relation: material-process, body-use, manufacturer, or cultural persona.
- Fashion and product design share world-building, silhouette, material, and myth.

### Digital/game/pop culture

Recovered pixel art, HUDs, design computing, Jamie Hewlett, Fira, and related visual culture rows. These reinforce that digital design is made of constraints: resolution, rendering, interface overlay, fictional worlds, asset pipelines, and distribution.

Rule: digital visual culture should be read through technical constraints plus cultural adoption.
