## Main additions

### Branding, magazines, wordmarks, and graphic canons

Landor Associates, Wallpaper magazine, Wordmark, Henry Wolf, Print magazine, Angus Hyland, Saul Bass, Branding the Barnes, and related rows strengthen the identity/graphic canon layer. They are mostly reference or biographical sources, but they clarify that design identity is carried through agencies, magazines, editorial art direction, wordmarks, and institutional redesign projects.

Rule: graphic design history is partly a history of distribution channels — magazines, agencies, awards, hall-of-fame canons, and public-facing identity systems.

### Accessibility and visual language

ColorADD and The Design of Symbols-adjacent rows strengthen symbol/accessibility thinking. ColorADD is important because it treats color identification as an accessibility/language problem rather than merely a palette issue.

Rule: accessibility design should create shareable symbolic systems, not just post-hoc accommodations.

### Overengineering and decision matrices

Overengineering and decision-matrix method add design-process vocabulary. Overengineering is a failure mode where complexity exceeds actual requirements; decision matrices help compare options against explicit criteria.

Rule: good design judgment needs both negative diagnosis and selection structure: know when something is too much, and know how tradeoffs are being evaluated.

### Typography and type detail

Romain du Roi/calligraphy, IPA transcription fonts, Just Another Foundry size-specific type adjustments, Numero sign, Lucida/Fira/DejaVu-related rows, Vietnamese punctuation, and multilingual Wikipedia duplicates all strengthen the type infrastructure thread.

Rule: type is not one global skin. It is script, size, rendering, punctuation, transcription, localization, and cultural convention.

### UI / TUI / Win32 graphic elements

The TUI basics and Win32 graphic elements rows add low-level interface vocabulary. They are not contemporary UI theory, but they are useful reminders that interface design has platform primitives: controls, states, drawing conventions, constraints, and input/output models.

Rule: interface aesthetics are constrained by component semantics and platform behavior before visual styling begins.

### Architecture and spatial design

Doshi Levien, David Mellor, Matthew Williamson, Vivienne Westwood, Archigram, Richard Rogers, Reginald Mitchell, Ron Arad, Hussein Chalayan, Droog, David Chipperfield, Robert Wilson, Norman Foster, Future Systems, Sebastian Bergne, Müller+Hess, Copenhagen Opera House, SHH/Evita Peroni, and Henning Larsen/e-architect rows extend architecture/product/fashion/cultural design coverage.

Rules:

- Archigram, Future Systems, Rogers, Foster, Price, and related sources reinforce architecture as technology, infrastructure, speculation, and enabling system.
- Fashion/design figures such as Westwood, Chalayan, Williamson, Treacy, Galliano, McQueen show that design identity often becomes body, performance, and persona.
- Retail/interior concepts are spatial branding: store design controls movement, attention, merchandise hierarchy, and memory.

### Information design history

`Who Gives The Best Info? A Short History Of Information Design` reinforces the earlier information-design line: clarity, comparison, annotation, diagrams, and legibility are cultural tools, not just technical formatting.

Rule: information design is public reasoning made visible.
