## New or strengthened topics

### Fernando + Humberto Campana

The Campana brothers strengthen the reuse/material-improvisation thread. Their design language often turns vernacular, waste, craft, and informal construction into high-design furniture. The value is not “recycled look” but transformation of low-status material systems into expressive structure.

Rule: material elevation works when the original material logic remains legible instead of being disguised.

### Jasper Morrison

Morrison represents quiet, super-normal product design: objects should become useful companions rather than visual spectacles. This complements Industrial Facility and Rams: restraint is not absence, but a disciplined refusal of unnecessary performance.

Rule: normality can be designed; it requires judging what should disappear and what must remain legible.

### Terence Conran

Conran matters as design retail/patronage infrastructure. He helped make modern domestic design accessible through shops, publishing, restaurants, and institutions. This is not only a biography; it shows that design culture spreads through distribution and taste-making systems.

Rule: design literacy often scales through retail, publishing, and institutions, not only through objects.

### Abram Games

Games’ “maximum meaning, minimum means” is a concise rule for poster and information graphics. The goal is not minimalism as style but compression: image, message, memory, and action in one economical visual proposition.

Rule: reduce until meaning sharpens, not until the artifact becomes empty.

### Richard Sweeney, Ed Swan, Luis Eslava

These exhibition/installation figures extend the material-experiment cluster: paper structure, optical/obscura views, face-to-face spatial interventions. Their importance is evidence of mid-2000s design culture valuing experimental material behavior and exhibition experience.

Rule: exhibition design can be a laboratory for material perception, not only a display surface.

### Kerr Noble and Derek Birdsall

Kerr Noble and Derek Birdsall add to the British graphic-design line. Birdsall especially reinforces editorial/identity craft, typographic judgment, and long-form graphic intelligence. Kerr Noble fits the European graphic/design culture context.

Rule: graphic design maturity often appears in editorial systems and typographic pacing, not just single posters or logos.

### BarberOsgerby, Ronan + Erwan Bouroullec, Hella Jongerius

These strengthen contemporary product/furniture design: industrial clarity, domestic softness, craft-material hybridity, and manufacturer collaboration. Jongerius especially shows color/material research as serious industrial design knowledge.

Rule: product design is a negotiation between manufacturer capability, material research, domestic behavior, and object character.

### Philip Treacy, John Galliano, Jamie Hewlett, M/M, Timorous Beasties

This group broadens the corpus into fashion, illustration, graphic authorship, pattern, and pop culture. Hewlett’s character/world-building and M/M’s graphic-fashion-art crossover matter because design identity can be carried by fictional worlds, campaigns, typography, and cultural persona.

Rule: visual identity can live as a character/world system, not only a corporate system.

### Flaminio Bertoni, Cedric Price, Tim Berners-Lee

Bertoni adds automotive sculpture and the Citroën lineage: vehicle form as cultural/technical leap. Cedric Price adds anti-monumental, flexible, time-based architecture; buildings as enabling systems rather than fixed monuments. Berners-Lee adds the web as one of the largest design infrastructures: protocols, access, linking, and openness as design conditions.

Rule: some of the most important design is infrastructural and enabling; it creates conditions for others to design, publish, move, and connect.

### DejaVu fonts

DejaVu extends the open-source typography cluster: font coverage, libre licensing, and Unicode/script access are part of digital inclusion.

Rule: typography infrastructure is political and practical: who can write, render, localize, and participate.

## Consolidated additions

1. Archive sweeps increasingly recover provenance more than new theory; avoid over-weighting duplicates.
2. Design culture spreads through retail, publication, institutions, and infrastructure.
3. Contemporary product design often lives between quiet normality, craft-material research, and manufacturer collaboration.
4. Fashion/graphic/pop design can operate through worlds, personas, and cultural codes.
5. Open web and open fonts are design infrastructure, not neutral technical background.
