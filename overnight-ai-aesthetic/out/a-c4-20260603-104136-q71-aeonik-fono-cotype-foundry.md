Designed for typographers seeking that “mono vibe,” it blends the best of classical Aeonik and Aeonik Mono into a proportional type family that’s a pleasure to use in text and headlines alike. With proportional spacing and kerning, Aeonik Fono is easier to read than traditional monospaced fonts while maintaining the mono-inspired feel. It is available in eight weights, from Thin to Black, and supports an extensive range of languages, including most European languages written in the Latin script, as well as Vietnamese, multiple Cyrillic-based languages, and Greek. Language support options can be selected at checkout. Just like Aeonik, Aeonik Fono features closed apertures, perpendicular stroke endings, and clean, well-defined letterforms. To evoke the mono aesthetic, long serifs have been added to letters like ‘i,’ ‘j,’ ‘l,’ and ‘I.’ It also brings a playful twist to the Aeonik family with boxed and circled numeral sets, as well as alternates for characters like ‘a,’ ‘f,’ ‘y,’ ‘J,’ ‘Q,’ ‘7,’ and ‘&.’ Three arrow sets – small arrows, uppercase arrows, and navigational arrows – further expand its versatility. Ideal for editorial and branding projects, Aeonik Fono offers more dynamic proportions and oversized serifs, taking the grotesque genre into fresh territory. It pairs seamlessly with the original Aeonik, making it perfect for projects that demand a creative or complex typographic hierarchy.
